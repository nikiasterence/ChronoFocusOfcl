How to Improve Your Speed

  • Focus on accuracy before speed; speed will follow naturally.
  • Maintain proper posture with your back straight and wrists level.
  • Keep your fingers on the home row (ASDF JKL;) as a base.
  • Practice consistently every day, even for a few minutes.
  • Avoid looking at the keyboard to build muscle memory faster.

The journey to becoming a proficient typist is one of dedication and consistent effort. It's not about achieving blistering speed overnight, but rather about the gradual refinement of muscle memory and technique. Each practice session builds upon the last, strengthening the neural pathways that connect your brain to your fingertips. Remember to maintain a relaxed posture, allowing your fingers to move gracefully across the keyboard. Don't be discouraged by mistakes; they are an integral part of the learning process. Embrace each error as an opportunity to identify a weakness and correct it. Over time, you will find that the letters you once had to search for now flow effortlessly from your thoughts to the screen.
